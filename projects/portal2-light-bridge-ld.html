Overview

A four-chamber map that treats the Light Bridge as a single mechanic with a full vocabulary. Each puzzle adds one verb — portal interaction, object carry, player redirection — escalating in the order players would expect, then breaking the expectation.

Contributions

  1. Single-mechanic study — four chambers that explore the Light Bridge from basic to apex use.
  2. Progressive difficulty — concepts introduced in logical order, with one new verb per chamber.
  3. Iterative playtesting — tested and rebuilt until each puzzle had exactly one intended solution path.
Chapter 01

Key Goals

Concept — four puzzles, one mechanic

One mechanic, fully scoped

The design thesis — explore the mechanic. The entire level orbits a single idea so players discover and master its applications before the level is over.

  1. Explore the mechanic — the full level orbits a single idea so players discover and master its applications.
  2. Progressive challenge — concepts introduced in logical sequence; difficulty rises alongside creative physics play.
Chapter 02

Design Process

Puzzle 01 — Lightbridge & portal interaction

01 · Intro

Basic Lightbridge & portal interaction. Teaches the player the surface nature of the bridge — solid from one side, a highway from the other.

Puzzles 02–03 — object manipulation

02 · Build

Object manipulation under bridge constraints. The bridge becomes a platform — cubes balance on it, roll off it, and block it.

Puzzle 04 — player movement manipulation

03 · Apex

Player movement manipulation — the bridge as a traversal tool rather than a wall. The final chamber inverts expectation and asks the player to ride their own geometry.

Iterative playtesting

04 · Playtest

Each chamber went through rebuilds until one intended solution remained. Unintended shortcuts were either closed off or promoted to the canonical path.
